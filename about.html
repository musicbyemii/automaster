How It Works

Understanding the AutoMaster processing chain

AutoMaster applies a series of audio processing to your tracks entirely in your browser. Here's a detailed breakdown of what happens when you master your audio:

1

Audio Analysis & Normalization

When you upload your audio, AutoMaster first analyzes the overall loudness using RMS (root mean square) calculation. It then normalizes the audio to a standard level for consistent processing.

This ensures your track will have a balanced starting point before applying other mastering effects.

Technical Details

The analyzer calculates RMS loudness and applies gain compensation. The audio is decoded into a raw PCM format at its original sample rate (up to 192kHz) with 32-bit floating point precision for all processing.

2

Frequency Balancing

AutoMaster applies a multi-stage equalizer to enhance your mix's frequency balance:

  • High-pass filter removes unnecessary low-end rumble below 28Hz with a steep slope (Q=4.0)
  • Low-pass filter gently tames frequencies above 16.5kHz
  • Bass boost (+3dB at 100Hz lowshelf)
  • Mid boost (+2dB at 1kHz peaking)
  • Adaptive treble boost (1-7dB at 4kHz highshelf based on high frequency content analysis)
Technical Details

Uses Web Audio API's BiquadFilterNodes with carefully tuned parameters. The adaptive treble boost analyzes spectral energy above 4kHz and applies compensation based on the detected high frequency content.

3

Dynamic Control

A professional-grade compressor smooths out volume fluctuations:

  • Threshold at -24dB
  • Knee at 10dB for smooth compression
  • Ratio of 3:1 (stable mode) or 4:1 (aggressive mode)
  • Fast attack (10ms) to catch transients
  • Musical release (250ms) for natural decay
Technical Details

Uses Web Audio API's DynamicsCompressorNode with carefully tuned parameters. The compression is applied after EQ to ensure frequency-balanced dynamics control.

4

Stereo Imaging

AutoMaster analyzes and optimizes your stereo field:

  • Stereo correlation analysis to detect phase issues
  • Adaptive stereo adjustment - if stereo width is too extreme (>0.8 correlation), brings it 75% towards center
  • StereoPannerNode used for precise stereo control
Technical Details

Calculates inter-channel correlation coefficient and applies mid-side processing when needed. Uses StereoPannerNode with adaptive panning values based on L/R channel difference analysis.

5

Final Limiting & Export

After all processing, AutoMaster applies final adjustments:

  • Loudness normalization to streaming standard levels
  • 16-bit conversion with proper dithering
  • WAV format with correct headers and metadata

The result is a professionally mastered track ready for distribution, all without ever uploading your audio to any server.

Technical Details

Uses gain staging with multiple passes through the Web Audio API's OfflineAudioContext for sample-accurate processing. Final output is rendered to 16-bit WAV format with proper headers and metadata.
